New Zealand

We were lucky enough to spend five weeks in New Zealand and we totally fell in love. We encountered beaches with palm trees and snow capped mountains, it really does have it all.

We were blessed with fantastic weather the whole time that we were there and got to experience so many different things.

We enjoyed travelling around in our rental cars and shopping in the local supermarkets. By the end of the five weeks we almost felt like we lived there and I really didnâ€™t want to leave.

Stops

Auckland

After a night flight from Perth we were very tired when we arrived but unfortunately we had another four hours before we could check into the hostel so we ended up going to the cinema.

The following day we took a walk up Mount Eden where there were nice views of the city followed by some drinks down by the harbour.

Bay of Islands

We had to take a car ferry to get to our accommodation here. It was a lovely little chalet with canoes and access to a little private pebble beach.

It was here that we did our first of many hikes, just a short one to get us started but it still had amazing views.

Ararimu

This was a back to basics stop where we stayed in a tiny timber A frame hut. The toilet was a short walk down the forest path and there was no electricity let alone wifi! We had a good game of scrabble and walked deep into the forest in the evening to see the glow worms.

Coromandel

Here we visited Cathedral Cove which is accessible only by foot or sea. Although it was a lovely place, the other tourists made it impossible to get the same peaceful pictures that you see on the internet.

It was here that we also went to one of the most surreal beaches of the trip - Hot Water Beach. There were certain areas where you could dig a hole and when you reached water it was hot. Sometimes it was even so hot that it burnt!

Rotorua

The highest commercially rafted waterfall is just outside Rotorua and we had to try it out. It was great fun only one of us fell out.

The town itself has a bit of a funny smell as there is a lot of geothermal activity so we also visited some nearby naturally warm streams and waterfalls.

There are also lots of redwood trees where they have created a tree walk high up in the trees.

Tongariro National Park

The Tongariro Alpine Crossing was our first big hike. We had to get up early to get the shuttle bus to the start. There were sections with lots of steps and it was very windy at the top. There are beautiful blue lakes along the track which are like nothing that you have ever seen before.
