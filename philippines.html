Philippines

We originally wanted to spend at least another week in the Philippines but while we were there the government imposed a lot of restrictions on travel. We ended up spending a lot of our free time rearranging flights and planning our next best move which was a little stressful at times.

The Philippines is made up of so many small islands and we still have a lot left to explore. When we finally get to go back we would love to take a diving course as the area is home to a whole host of marine life that we werenâ€™t able to see this time around.

Stops

Mt Pinatubo

We stayed close to the airport in a city called Angeles which was well located for a trip to Mt Pinatubo. We had to get up early as it was a long drive followed by a long dusty jeep ride to the foot of the trail. We walked to see the crater lake which was formed when the volcano erupted in 1991.

Coron

The hotel we stayed in was the fanciest one of the trip. We stayed in little stone cottages where the bathroom had a view of the sea. We had a lovely time here canoeing and paddle boarding and we took a day trip on a bangka boat around some of the smaller local islands. We made stops where we could snorkel and swim and the crew cooked us a lovely fish lunch.

El Nido

There were so many amazing sunsets in El Nido and we spent a couple of days just relaxing on the local beaches. We also took another bangka boat tour where we saw Snake Island where a strip of sand connects two islands at low tide.
