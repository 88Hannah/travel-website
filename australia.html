Australia

We visited Australia twice on our trip. First to Western Australia and then later to Queensland and New South Wales.

Western Australia was added to our itinerary quite late in the trip planning and mainly because we were able to find good flights from China and to New Zealand. We were surprised how much we liked it. We enjoyed the heat after a chilly stop in Shanghai and the scenery was lovely.

In Eastern Australia we rented a camper van which we drove from Brisbane to Sydney, making stops along the coast on the way. The sea was much warmer but the waves were rough!

Stops

Perth and Fremantle

We flew from Shanghai to Perth via Kuala Lumpur and then took a cheap bus to our hostel in Fremantle which is a little port city on the outskirts of Perth. It’s renowned for its architecture and has a lot of colonial-era buildings. We enjoyed eating and drinking outside and soaking in the atmosphere.

In Perth, we met up with my old friend who was a brilliant tour guide! We got great views of the city from his office, had a lovely ice cream walking around Elizabeth Quay, saw a big tree in King’s Park and visited Mundaring Weir dam.

Rottnest Island

We took a day trip to this small island just off the coast. It took us about 5 hours to cycle around along the coast road, making little stops along the way for a swim in the sea and to take pictures and selfies of the island's only native mammal - the little quokka!

Margaret River

This stop was added to our trip because we heard that it had both great wine and great beaches and it didn’t disappoint. We stayed on the outskirts of town in a little house where the neighbours were kangaroos! This is where we had our first of many BBQs. The sunsets were beautiful, especially with a glass of the local wine.

Bunbury

We travelled along the coast to Bunbury, via Busselton. It has a reputation as a good place to see dolphins and it didn’t disappoint. From here we headed to the airport for our flight to New Zealand.

Brisbane

Although very humid, we loved our short stay in Brisbane and wished that we had planned in more time here. The free river boat was a great way to see the city and the South Bank is definitely an area that we wanted to explore more. It will just have to wait until next time.

Byron Bay

After picking up our massive campervan in Brisbane we drove to Byron Bay for our first two nights. The weather was amazing and we took a hot walk out to the lighthouse before chilling on the beach and having a cool down in the sea. The place is full of young surfers which means there is a good atmosphere at all the bars in town in evenings.

Nambucca Heads

We found a free camping spot here which saved us some money. We woke up early to see the sunrise down at the beach and even had an early morning swim before heading off to our next stop.

Forster

For our last stop in the van we booked onto another campsite and were given a nice pitch near the BBQ area. The sea here was the roughest we’d come across so far and we all found it hard to stay on our feet when the waves hit! We hired a little motor boat to explore a bit further and found a nice beach with beautiful shallow water.

Cronulla

We stayed with family and it was lovely to see other people that we knew. We were treated to our own personal surf lesson and although we loved it, we found it exhausting. The sea was so powerful and we got knocked around a lot!

Sydney

It rained nearly the whole time that we were there! We met up with an old university friend for lunch before attempting to get around and see the sights. We saw most things but I hear that it all looks much nicer in the sunshine.
